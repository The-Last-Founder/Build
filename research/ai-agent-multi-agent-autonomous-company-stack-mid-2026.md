# The AI Agent / Multi-Agent / Autonomous-Company Enabling Stack (Mid-2026)

This document stores the research summary from issue [#47](https://github.com/The-Last-Founder/Build/issues/47).

## TL;DR

The “agent-orchestrated company” stack is real and layered:

- **Application layer**: Cofounder (cofounder.co), Paperclip, and AI-cofounder tools.
- **Orchestration layer**: LangGraph, CrewAI, Microsoft Agent Framework, OpenAI/Anthropic/Google SDKs.
- **Interoperability layer**: MCP, A2A, ACP, ANP, AP2/x402 (with consolidation momentum under the Linux Foundation’s Agentic AI Foundation).
- **Infrastructure layer**: runtime, memory, and observability systems.
- **Vertical AI employee layer**: Sierra, Decagon, Cognition/Devin.

The stack is strongly influenced by thought leaders and foundational papers, but current evidence suggests infrastructure is maturing faster than full autonomous-company apps.

## Key findings

1. **Most mature activity is still below the app layer**  
   Orchestration frameworks and interoperability protocols are further along than “run a full company autonomously” products.
2. **Interoperability governance is becoming a strategic bottleneck and unlock**  
   Protocol fragmentation is starting to consolidate, with governance now a major part of ecosystem health.
3. **Vertical AI employees show stronger production traction than full-stack AI-workforce OS tools**  
   Categories like AI customer support/sales agents appear more validated than all-in-one autonomous-company operating systems.
4. **Hype vs. reality is a first-order risk**  
   “Agent washing,” autonomy overclaims, and benchmark gaming are recurring patterns that should inform Build’s evaluation standards.

## Why this matters for Build

- Prioritize tools and workflows that are production-credible now.
- Treat end-to-end autonomous-company claims as hypotheses to test, not assumptions.
- Keep protocol interoperability and evaluation rigor visible in decision-making.
