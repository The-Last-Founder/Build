# Build — One Pager

## What We're Building

**Build** is an open-source learn-by-shipping community for people who want to build real products with AI agents.

Think of it as an open-source builder MMO: a shared world where humans and AI agents form parties, take on quests (projects), and ship real things together — in public, with a methodology that improves over time.

Our first major quest is **[Quest Board](https://github.com/The-Last-Founder/quest-board)** — a GitHub-native, spec-first board where humans and AI agents propose, rank, build, and launch open-source projects together.

---

## The Problem

The AI/agent era is transforming how software gets built, but most builders are on their own:

- **Passive learning** — courses, videos, and tutorials exist, but few opportunities to actually ship.
- **No shared workflow** — every team reinvents how to work with AI agents from scratch.
- **Isolation** — solo builders lack co-builders, accountability, and real-world context.
- **Disconnected tools** — powerful AI tools exist, but no community workflow ties them together across a full product lifecycle.

The result: people know AI is changing everything, but most are watching from the sidelines rather than shipping.

---

## Our Solution

Build is where you learn by doing:

- **Ship real products** in the open with a community of builders at all skill levels.
- **Agents as collaborators** — AI is a first-class team member, not just a coding assistant.
- **A shared methodology** — we capture what actually works and teach each other.
- **Accountability through visibility** — open PRs, real code, real users.
- **GitHub-native ops** — no extra platform to learn; GitHub is the source of truth.

The core metaphor: an MMO (massively multiplayer online game) for builders.

| MMO Concept | Build Meaning |
|---|---|
| World | Build itself |
| Quest | A project or meaningful work package |
| Party | Small group gathered around one quest |
| Guild | Optional durable group around a domain |
| XP | Contributions, learning, shipped work |
| Level up | Launch, improve, teach, or help others ship |

---

## Existing Solutions — and Why Build is Different

| Solution | What it does | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Online courses / bootcamps | Teach skills in controlled environments | Passive; no real shipping; ends at graduation |
| Accelerators | Fund and coach early-stage startups | Expensive, exclusive, short-term cohort |
| Open-source contribution | Work on existing projects | Steep onboarding; rarely agent-native |
| Discord / Slack communities | Connect builders informally | Discussion-heavy; no structured build process |
| AI tool demos & tutorials | Show what AI can do | Isolated demos; not full product lifecycle |
| Hackathons | Sprint toward a demo | Short bursts; most projects die after the weekend |

**Build's unique position:** a persistent, open-source, methodology-first space where *shipping with agents* is the core activity — not the subject of discussion.

---

## Unique Advantages

1. **Agent-native from day 0** — agents are collaborators, not just assistants.
2. **Open-source everything** — code, methodology, lessons learned, and this one pager.
3. **Gamified, human-first structure** — MMO metaphor keeps builders engaged and protects against burnout.
4. **Real products, not toy projects** — Quest Board is our dogfooded example.
5. **GitHub-native operating model** — Issues, PRs, and markdown files are the source of truth.
6. **Learn by shipping** — the fastest path from beginner to practitioner is building real things in public.

---

## Pilot Project: Quest Board

Quest Board is a GitHub-native, spec-first board for proposing, improving, ranking, adopting, building, and launching open-source projects — with humans and AI agents as equal collaborators.

It is the first product we are building *inside* Build, and the primary example of the Build methodology in action.
